This chart shows the percentage of adults currently married in the United States from 1920 to 2012. The data is presented as a bar graph with years on the x-axis and percentages on the y-axis.
Key points from the chart:
1. The highest percentage of married adults was in 1960 at 72.2%.
2. There's a general downward trend in marriage rates from 1960 onwards.
3. The most recent data point in 2012 shows 50.5% of adults were currently married.
4. The earliest data point in 1920 shows 65.0% of adults were married.
5. There was a slight increase in marriage rates between 1920 and 1960.
6. The steepest decline appears to be between 1980 and 2000.
The chart notes that it's based on adults age 18 and older, and "currently married" includes respondents reporting "married, spouse absent." Those reporting as "separated" are not included in the "currently married" category.
The data source is cited as Pew Research Center tabulations of the 1920-2000 Decennial Census data and 2006-12 American Community Survey Integrated Public Use Micro Samples.
