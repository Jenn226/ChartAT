The chart is a bar graph titled "Support for democratic principles". 
* It shows the percentage of people from 34 countries who find it very important to have certain democratic principles in their country. 
* The responses are ranked in descending order with the principle considered most important at the top.
* A fair judiciary tops the list with 82% finding it very important.
Following are the percentages for other principles in decreasing order of importance:
* Gender equality- 74%
* Free religion- 68%
* Regular elections- 65%
* Free speech-64%
* Press freedom-64%
* Freedom on the internet- 59%
* Human rights groups can operate freely- 55%
* Opposition parties can operate freely -54%
Note that the percentages are medians based on 34 countries surveyed in The Spring 2019 Global Attitudes Survey.
