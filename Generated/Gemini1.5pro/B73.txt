The chart shows results from a survey. It illustrates how U.S. adults' accuracy in identifying original reporting correlates with the importance they place on keeping up with the news.
* Those who find following the news "very important" were most likely to correctly identify original reporting sources at 55%.
*  Those who find it "somewhat important" came in at 44%. 
* "Not too/at all important" respondents were least likely at 32%.
