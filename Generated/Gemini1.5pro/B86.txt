The chart describes the age demographics of U.S. poll workers in the 2018 general election.
Key findings:
* A majority (58%) of U.S. poll workers in the 2018 general election were over the age of 60.
* 31% of poll workers were aged 61-70.
* 27% of poll workers were aged 71 or older.
Other age groups were represented as follows:
* 26% of poll workers were aged 41-60.
* 9% of poll workers were aged 26-40.
* 4% of poll workers were aged 18-25.
* 4% of poll workers were aged 17 and younger.
Note: The data is based on poll workers whose age was known. Not all jurisdictions reported poll worker age.
