The chart presents survey results about the difficulty that LGBT individuals had in telling their parents about their sexual orientation or gender identity. 
**Key Findings:**
* **Telling Father Perceived as Harder:** 65% of LGBT individuals found it difficult to tell their father, compared to 59% who found it difficult to tell their mother. 
* **Significant Difficulty Overall:**  A majority of respondents reported difficulty telling both their mother and father, indicating that coming out to parents is challenging for many LGBT individuals.
**Details:**
* The data is specifically from LGBT individuals who have already come out to their parents.
* The sample size for those who told their mother is 648, while the sample size for those who told their father is 435.
* The chart doesn't include data from individuals who haven't come out to their parents.
**Source:** Pew Research Center
