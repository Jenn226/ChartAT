The chart describes the percentage of US adults who owned different types of electronic devices in 2011. 
* **Cell phones** were most common, owned by 83% of adults.
* **Desktop computers** were next at 57%, followed closely by **laptops** at 56%.
* **DVRs** were owned by 52% and **MP3 players** by 44% of adults.
*  Far fewer adults owned **e-readers** (12%) or **tablets** (8%).
The data makes it clear that while cell phones, computers, and DVRs were common household items by 2011, e-readers and tablets were still niche products.
