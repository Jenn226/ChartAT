The line chart shows the response rates to telephone surveys conducted by Pew Research Center each year from 1997 to 2018. 
**Key takeaways:**
* **Overall declining trend:** Telephone survey response rates have fallen significantly from 36% in 1997 to just 6% in 2018.
* **Brief plateau:** There was a brief plateau between 2013 and 2016, where response rates remained steady at 9%. However, this was followed by another sharp drop in 2018.
**Other important information:**
* The response rate refers to the percentage of successful contacts out of all eligible households sampled.
* The surveys only included landline numbers between 1997 and 2006.
* The rates shown are typical for surveys conducted in each respective year.
This chart highlights the growing challenge of conducting telephone surveys in the face of changing communication habits and declining landline use.
