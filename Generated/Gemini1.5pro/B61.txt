The chart shows the percentage of women in the U.S. military from 1975 to 2017, broken down by enlisted personnel and commissioned officers. 
**Key findings:**
* **Overall Increase:** The percentage of women in both enlisted roles and as officers has grown significantly over the period examined.
* **Faster Growth for Officers:** While both groups saw increases, the percentage of women officers grew at a faster pace than the percentage of women enlisted personnel. 
* **2017 Figures:** By 2017, women constituted nearly 20% of commissioned officers and about 16% of enlisted personnel.
**Data specifics:**
| Year | Enlisted Women (%) |  Officer Women (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 1975 | 5 | 5 |
| 1985 | 10 | 10 |
| 1995 | 13 | 14 |
| 2005 | 14 | 14 |
| 2017 | 16 | 18 |
**Important Notes:**
* The data only encompasses the four main branches of the Department of Defense (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines). 
* "Officers" refers solely to commissioned officers.
**Source:** U.S. Department of Defense 2017 Population Representation in the Military Services published data.
