The bar graph shows that most legislators in five countries have Twitter accounts and tweeted during the study period from January to June, with percentages exceeding 70%. The data is sourced from an analysis of tweets by national-level legislators in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand between January 1st and June 30th, 2019. There were 2,180 legislators who had Twitter accounts, including 2,056 who posted at least one tweet. This information was published as part of "For Global Legislators on Twitter, an Engaged Minority Creates Outsize Share of Content" for PEW Research Center.
