The bar graph shows percentages of U.S. adults who believe that all Americans should have the right to have certain information permanently deleted by people or organizations holding such data, including potentially embarrassing photos and videos (87%), financial data collected by tax preparers (79%), medical data collected by health care providers (69%) and data collected by law enforcement agencies like criminal records and mugshots (36%). The source is PEW Research Center's survey conducted from June 3-17 in 2019. Those not giving an answer were excluded from this analysis.
