The graph shows that in 2019, many South Koreans were pessimistic about their political system. Specifically, 68% of respondents said they were pessimistic about how their political system works; 62% disagreed with the idea that most elected officials care what people think; and 49% believed that the state is not run for the benefit of its citizens. Additionally, 44% expressed dissatisfaction with Korean democracy as a whole. The data comes from the Spring 2019 Global Attitudes Survey by PEW Research Center.
