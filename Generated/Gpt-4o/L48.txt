The chart from the Pew Research Center depicts the percentage of Britons who have a favorable or unfavorable opinion of the EU from 2004 to 2020.
Here's a detailed description:
- **Time Period**: The x-axis shows the years from 2004 to 2020.
- **Percentage**: The y-axis ranges from 0% to 100%.
- **Favorable Opinions**: Represented by the green line.
- In 2004, 54% had a favorable opinion.
- This percentage fluctuated slightly over the years, dropping to 46% in 2012.
- Post-2016, the percentage varied around the mid-40s.
- By 2020, the favorable view reached 60%.
- **Unfavorable Opinions**: Represented by the blue line.
- In 2004, 36% had an unfavorable opinion.
- This percentage gradually increased, reaching 45% by 2012.
- After the 2016 referendum, unfavorable views fluctuated around the mid-40s as well.
- By 2020, the percentage dropped to 37%.
- A notable point is the **June 2016 EU membership referendum**, marked by a vertical dashed line. After this point, the favorable opinions rose significantly, while unfavorable opinions declined.
- **Survey Details**: The data comes from the Summer 2020 Global Attitudes Survey conducted from June 10 to July 28, 2020 (Q8d).
**Note**: The chart also mentions that those who did not answer are not shown.
