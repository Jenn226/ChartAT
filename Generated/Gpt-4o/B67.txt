The chart titled "India's Greatest National Threat" presents the responses of Indians to a survey question about what they perceive to be the greatest threat to their nation. The data is expressed in percentages and here are the specific details:
- 41% of respondents identified Pakistan as the greatest national threat.
- 19% of respondents identified Lashkar-e-Taiba as the greatest national threat.
- 16% of respondents identified Naxalites as the greatest national threat.
- 5% of respondents identified China as the greatest national threat.
- 3% of respondents selected "All/None of the above."
- 17% of respondents either didn't know or refused to answer.
This information is sourced from the Spring 2012 Global Attitudes Survey, as indicated at the bottom of the chart. The chart is from the Pew Research Center.
