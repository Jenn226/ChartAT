The chart titled "About seven-in-ten say the census will produce an accurate population count" illustrates the results of a survey indicating public opinion on the expected accuracy of the 2020 U.S. Census in counting the number of people living in the United States.
Here are the specific data points presented in the pie chart:
- **Somewhat successful:** 60% of respondents believe the census will be somewhat successful at accurately counting the population.
- **Not too successful:** 26% of respondents think it will be not too successful.
- **Very successful:** 8% of respondents believe it will be very successful.
- **Not at all successful:** 5% of respondents think it will be not at all successful.
- **No answer:** 1% of respondents did not provide an answer.
The survey was conducted by the Pew Research Center from June 16 to June 22, 2020.
