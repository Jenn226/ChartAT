The chart titled "U.S. unauthorized immigrant total rises, then falls" depicts the estimated number of unauthorized immigrants in the United States from 1990 to 2017.
Key data points are as follows:
- In 1990, the estimated number of unauthorized immigrants was 3.5 million.
- By 1995, this number increased to 5.7 million.
- In 2000, the estimated number reached 8.6 million.
- It continued to rise to 10.7 million in 2005.
- By 2010, the number peaked at 12.2 million.
- From 2010 to 2017, the trend shows a gradual decline:
- 11.4 million in 2011
- 11.1 million in 2013
- 11.0 million in 2015
- 10.7 million in 2016
- 10.5 million in 2017
The chart also includes a shaded region representing the range of the estimated 90% confidence interval. The data source is the Pew Research Center, which based its estimates on augmented U.S. Census Bureau data.
