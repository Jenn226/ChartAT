The chart is titled "Despite recent uptick, U.S.-Mexico border apprehensions still far below late 1990s levels" and depicts the number of apprehensions at the southwest border of the U.S. by fiscal year from 1998 to 2018. The source of the data is U.S. Customs and Border Protection, and it was published by Pew Research Center.
Key points from the chart:
- The number of apprehensions peaked in fiscal year 2000 at 1,643,679.
- There was a sharp decline after 2000, dropping to 905,065 in 2003.
- The apprehensions continued to decrease, reaching 327,577 in 2011.
- Following the low in 2011, there was a fluctuating increase, with apprehensions rising to 396,579 in 2018.
The chart illustrates that despite the recent increase in apprehensions, the levels in 2018 are still significantly lower than the peak levels in the late 1990s and early 2000s.
