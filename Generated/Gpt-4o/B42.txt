The chart depicts the percentage of married couples where the husband is the primary earner (breadwinner) and the wife is a homemaker, categorized by the age of the wife.
- Overall, 24% of married couples follow this model.
- For wives aged 18-29, 32% of marriages have a breadwinner husband and a homemaker wife.
- For wives aged 30-49, the percentage is 27%.
- In the age group of 50-64, the percentage decreases to 22%.
- For wives aged 65 and older, only 14% of marriages follow this traditional model.
The data is based on an analysis by the Pew Research Center of the 2011 American Community Survey data from IPUMS, with employment status measured as of the previous week.
