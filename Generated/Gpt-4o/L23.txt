The chart shows the percentage of the U.S. population that is foreign-born from 1850 to 2017. Key data points and events are highlighted:
1. The percentage of foreign-born population in 1850 is recorded at 9.7%.
2. There is a significant rise, with the percentage peaking at 14.8% in 1890.
3. This percentage fluctuates and starts to decline after 1910.
4. The 1924 National Origins Quota Act is pointed out, after which the foreign-born population percentage declines.
5. The lowest point is reached in 1970 at 4.7%.
6. The 1965 Immigration and Nationality Act is noted, after which the percentage gradually increases.
7. By 2017, the percentage of the foreign-born population is 13.6%.
