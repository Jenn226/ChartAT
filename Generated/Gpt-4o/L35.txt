The chart details the decline in the U.S. teen birth rate over time, measuring births per 1,000 females aged 15-19 from 1940 to 2018. Key points include:
- **1940**: The teen birth rate was 54.1 births per 1,000 females.
- **1957**: During the baby boom years, the rate peaked at 96.3 births per 1,000 females.
- **1991**: A slight resurgence is visible, with the rate at 61.8 births per 1,000 females.
- **2008**: The rate drops to 41.5 births per 1,000 females.
- **2018**: The rate dramatically lowers to 17.4 births per 1,000 females.
It is noteworthy that the chart also emphasizes the baby boom years, marked clearly for reference.
Additional notes:
- The data includes live births only and excludes miscarraiges, stillbirths, and abortions.
- The youngest age of teens included is 15 years old.
The data were sourced from the National Center for Health Statistics, as published by the Pew Research Center.
