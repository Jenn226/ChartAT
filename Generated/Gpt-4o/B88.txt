The chart shows the percentage of U.S. adults who believe that global climate change is currently affecting their local community either a great deal or some. The data is categorized by the distance individuals live from a coastline.
- Overall, 59% of U.S. adults say climate change is affecting their local community.
- For those living less than 25 miles from a coastline, 67% believe climate change is affecting their community.
- Among those living 25-299 miles from a coastline, the percentage is 59%.
- For those living 300+ miles from the coastline, 50% say climate change is impacting their community.
This data was gathered from a survey conducted between March 27 and April 9, 2018, by the Pew Research Center.
