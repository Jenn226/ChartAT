The chart represents the percentage of Indians who perceive different entities as India's greatest national threat. Here are the specifics:
1. Pakistan is viewed by 41% of Indians as the greatest national threat.
2. Lashkar-e-Taiba follows with 19%.
3. Naxalites are considered a threat by 16%.
4. China is seen as a threat by only 5% of respondents.
5. A small percentage, 3%, believe that all or none of the above entities pose the greatest threat.
6. Additionally, 17% of the surveyed individuals either did not know or refused to answer.
The source of this data is cited as the Spring 2012 Global Attitudes Survey, question Q128 from the Pew Research Center.
