The chart represents the percentage of Pakistanis who perceive different entities as their country's greatest national threat. Here are the specifics:
1. India is viewed by 59% of Pakistanis as the greatest national threat.
2. The Taliban is seen as a threat by 23%.
3. al Qaeda is considered a threat by only 4%.
4. A combined 7% either believe all of the above, none of the above, or have no opinion/refused to answer.
The source of this data is cited as the "Spring 2012 Global Attitudes Survey" from the Pew Research Center.
