The image is a pie chart from the Pew Research Center, based on a survey conducted between October 1-13, 2019, regarding "U.S. Public Views on Climate and Energy." It represents the percentage of U.S. adults who believe that global climate change is affecting their local community.
The chart shows four categories:
1. **A great deal** - 22% of U.S. adults say climate change is affecting their local community a great deal.
2. **Some** - 39% indicate that it is having some impact.
3. **Not too much/not at all** - 38% believe that climate change is not having a significant effect on their community.
4. **No answer** - 1% did not provide an answer to this question.
